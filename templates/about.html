About Kelvin

Entirely student run Symphony Orchestra

The Kelvin Symphony Orchestra is an orchestra run be a team of roughly 10-15 committee members. These committee members work to ensure Kelvin can continue to run to keep the arts going for university students who otherwise wouldn't have the chance.

The vast majority if the members are not music students but from other disciplines the likes of the sciences, engineering and the humanities. We also have members from other universities in the Glasgow area like the University of Strathclyde and the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland (RCS)

Every year we run an audition cycle during the first few weeks of the academic year. Please view the join page for more information.

Wide, varied repertoire

Every year the committee choose with the help of the conductor for the season the repertoire. Our past repertoire has included the works of Tchaikovsky, Antonín Dvořák, Georges Bizet, Copland and more.

A full list of our previous concerts can be found on the Past Concerts page.

Beautiful Venue

We at the University of Glasgow are lucky to have access to world class facilities. One of these facilities is the beautiful Bute Hall.

Often used for graduations, this amazing space gives us the perfect venue to perform in, with the perfect environment for making music.

We thank the university for allowing us to use the space.
